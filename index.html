Teach a new code

  1. Type the word or phrase this movement will mean, then click Record movement.
  2. Cover your face with your right hand (the big R) and hold for the 3-2-1 countdown. Recording starts at zero.
  3. Perform your movement, then cover your face with your left hand (the big L) and hold for the 3-2-1 countdown to stop and save.

The hand-to-face moments are trimmed automatically, so only your movement is saved. Record the same word more than once to add examples and make it easier to recognize.

Saved codes

Everything is stored locally in your browser. Click a word (or Play all) to dance along with the ghost skeleton. When a word has several examples, each one can be played or deleted on its own.

    About Queer Coded

    Queer Coded turns body movement into words. You teach the app a movement bound to a word or phrase, then perform it to make the word appear and be spoken.

    Your privacy

    Everything happens locally, in your browser, on your device.

    • No data is sent to us. There is no server, no account, and no analytics. We never see your camera, your movements, or your words.
    • Your camera stays on your device. Video is processed frame by frame in the browser to detect body pose and is never uploaded, recorded, or stored. No video ever leaves your computer.
    • Only coordinates are saved. A saved code is just a short sequence of skeleton coordinates, stored in your browser's local storage. No images.
    • You are in control. Export or delete your codes anytime in the Codes tab. Clearing your browser storage removes everything.
    • Third-party models. The pose models (MediaPipe BlazePose, and optionally MoveNet or YOLO) download once from public CDNs so detection can run on your device. That is a normal file download; your camera data is not part of it.

    How it works

    Pose tracking runs in the browser (WebAssembly / WebGPU). Movements are compared with Dynamic Time Warping so tempo does not matter, and matching happens entirely on your device.
